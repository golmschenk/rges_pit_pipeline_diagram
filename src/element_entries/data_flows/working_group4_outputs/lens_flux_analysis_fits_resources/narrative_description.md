GBTDS data over the course of the mission will enable the detection of the host stars for planetary microlensing events as they move toward and away from the host stars for planetary microlensing events. The detection and measurement of the host stars magnitudes and the lens-source relative proper motion, $\mu$-rel, will help to constrain the mass and distance of the host star, and these constraints will be used for the RGES image-constrained microlensing modeling effort. MSOS provide lens-flux measurements determined from the (~weekly) periodic image stacks, but RGES will do its lens-flux analysis with the individual images in order to avoid any loss of angular resolution caused by the image stacking procedure. In the first few GBTDS seasons, the Lens Flux constraints will primarily be upper limits on the lens star brightness, and it will be difficult to remove (non-microlensing) parallax effects from apparent $\mu$-rel measurents until data from the first three GBTDS seasons are available. However, it is unlikely that with the lens-flux analysis using the data from the first three GBTDS seasons will be ready for release by the end of the second year of Roman’s prime mission.
