Maps characterize the rate of false negatives. The MSOS detection efficiencies will not be available until the end of the GBTDS. The PIT will provide early-look detection efficiencies at different fidelity levels:
* Pixel-Level Analysis: Shallow but sufficient coverage across event magnitudes to constrain signal-to-noise losses between images and light curves.
* Flux-Level Analysis: Deep, comprehensive analysis producing maps over mass ratio (q), separation (s), and angle (α) parameter space 	
  * First Season Deliverable: Global q, s map over all events
  * Three Seasons Deliverable: Comprehensive q, s, α maps for each event, targeting grids of 100 q (from 10⁻⁶ to 10⁻¹) × 100 s (from 0.1 to 6 θE) × 360 α (from 1° to 360°)
  * Additional statistics for detection efficiency maps
* As well as providing early-look detection efficiencies of the simple point-source point-lens (PSPL) case, the PIT will provide some preliminary detection efficiencies for cases with higher-order effects such as Limb-darkening and multi-planet systems
